Governance

The Government of Bloom

The Republic of Bloom is governed by an elected Congress of seven members chosen by citizens every two months. Congress elects a Speaker who serves as Head of State. A permanent President holds executive authority over Bloom's namelayer groups and constitutional matters. Together, these three branches uphold the rule of law and the will of Bloomeans.

Three Branches
Legislature
The Congress

Seven elected members serving two-month terms. Citizens vote in person at the Capitol Building using signed books. Congress passes laws by simple majority, may propose constitutional amendments by two-thirds majority, and holds impeachment power over any official including the Speaker. A vacant seat triggers a special election within three days.

Head of State
The Speaker

Elected by Congress from among its members by simple majority. The Speaker presides over all trials, issues Official Acts for temporary matters, and has final approval or denial power over all proposals after voting concludes. The Speaker may be removed by a two-thirds congressional vote with presidential approval. Term mirrors Congress — two months.

Executive
The President

A permanent role currently held by Slushhi. The President holds ownership of all Bloom namelayer groups, may veto constitutional amendments, and may block impeachments. The President is eligible to serve in Congress and hold additional offices like any citizen, but holds limited day-to-day governing power.

Bill of Rights
I

The right to free and fair elections, exempt from bribery or intimidation.

II

The right to a speedy, fair, and public trial with legal counsel of the accused's choosing or provided by the state.

III

The right to peacefully assemble in public spaces or on private properties with the owner's consent.

IV

Freedom of religion, free from state persecution, so long as it does not infringe upon the rights of others.

V

Freedom from slavery, indentured servitude, or involuntary servitude in any form.

—

These rights apply to all Bloomeans of Resident rank or higher and may not be abridged by any law or edict.
